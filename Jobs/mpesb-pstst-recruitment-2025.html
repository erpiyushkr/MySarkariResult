About MPESB and Primary School Teacher Role

What is MPESB?

The Madhya Pradesh Employees Selection Board (MPESB), also known as MP Vyapam, is the state recruitment agency responsible for conducting recruitment examinations and selection processes for various government departments in Madhya Pradesh. Established to ensure transparent and merit-based selections, MPESB conducts exams for teaching and non-teaching positions across the state.

Role of Primary School Teacher (PST)

Primary School Teachers are responsible for educating children in grades 1 to 5. Key responsibilities include:

  • Planning and delivering lessons according to curriculum
  • Assessing students' progress and providing feedback
  • Creating a positive learning environment
  • Developing teaching materials and resources
  • Maintaining classroom discipline
  • Participating in school activities and parent-teacher meetings
  • Identifying and supporting students with special needs

Career Insight:

A Primary School Teacher position offers a stable and rewarding career in education with opportunities for professional development and promotions to higher teaching positions or administrative roles in the education department.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is there any interview for MPESB PSTST selection?

No, there is no interview for MPESB PSTST selection. The selection is based solely on the written examination marks. Candidates who qualify the written exam will be called for document verification.

2. Can candidates from other states apply for MPESB PSTST?

Yes, candidates from other states can apply for MPESB PSTST. However, they will be considered under the General/Other State category and won't be eligible for MP state reservation benefits. The application fee for Other State candidates is ₹560/-.

3. What is the probation period for MP Primary Teachers?

The probation period for MP Primary Teachers is typically 2 years. During this period, candidates undergo orientation training and their performance is evaluated. Confirmation in service is subject to satisfactory completion of probation.

4. Is B.Ed required for MP Primary Teacher recruitment?

No, B.Ed is not required for Primary Teacher (Class 1-5) positions. The required qualifications are either Diploma in Elementary Education (D.El.Ed) or Bachelor of Elementary Education (B.El.Ed) as mentioned in the eligibility criteria.

5. What is the selection ratio for MPESB PSTST?

The selection ratio varies each year based on vacancies and applicants. Typically, about 5-8% of applicants are finally selected. The selection is purely merit-based, with cutoff marks varying by category.

6. What is the training period for selected candidates?

Selected candidates undergo a mandatory training program of 6 months at District Institute of Education and Training (DIET) centers. The training includes:

  • Pedagogical techniques
  • Classroom management
  • Curriculum implementation
  • Child psychology
  • Evaluation methods

7. Can male candidates apply for Primary Teacher positions?

Yes, male candidates can absolutely apply for Primary Teacher positions. There are no gender-based restrictions in the recruitment process. Male and female candidates receive the same salary and benefits.

8. What is the posting policy for MP Primary Teachers?

Selected candidates can be posted anywhere in Madhya Pradesh based on vacancy positions. The initial posting is typically in rural or semi-urban government primary schools. Candidates must serve in the allocated school for a minimum period (usually 3-5 years) before requesting transfer.

9. Is there any reservation for female candidates?

Yes, Madhya Pradesh government provides 30% horizontal reservation for female candidates across all categories. This means 30% of seats in each category (General, OBC, SC, ST) are reserved for women candidates from that category.

10. What are the career progression opportunities?

MP Primary Teachers have a structured career path:

  1. Primary Teacher (Grade III)
  2. Head Teacher (after 5-8 years of service)
  3. Block Resource Coordinator
  4. District Education Officer (through promotion exams)
Teachers can also pursue higher qualifications like B.Ed, M.Ed for career advancement and can appear for state and national level teacher eligibility tests for higher grades.
