Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the difference between ASRB NET and ARS?

Key differences:

  • NET: Eligibility test for Lecturer/Assistant Professor in Agricultural Universities
  • ARS: Competitive exam for scientist positions in ICAR institutes
  • NET is qualifying while ARS leads to direct recruitment
2. Can I apply for both NET and ARS together?

Yes, candidates can apply for both exams simultaneously:

  • Single application form for multiple exams
  • Combined fee payment option available
  • Need to fulfill eligibility criteria for both
3. What is the syllabus for ASRB exams?

Syllabus includes:

  1. General Agriculture: Basic agriculture principles
  2. Discipline Specific: Advanced subject knowledge
  3. Current Affairs: Recent developments in agriculture
4. Is there any interview for ARS selection?

Yes, ARS selection process includes:

  • Preliminary Exam (Objective)
  • Main Exam (Descriptive)
  • Interview/Viva-Voce (100 marks)
5. What are the career growth opportunities?

Promotional hierarchy:

  • ARS: Scientist B → Senior Scientist → Principal Scientist
  • SMS/STO: SMS/STO → Senior SMS/STO → Head of Department
  • Opportunities for foreign training and research collaborations
6. How many attempts are allowed?

Attempt limits:

  • NET: No limit
  • ARS: 4 attempts for General, 7 for OBC, No limit for SC/ST
  • SMS/STO: As per age limit
7. What documents are required during application?

Essential documents:

  • Scanned photograph and signature
  • Master's degree mark sheets
  • Caste certificate (if applicable)
  • Age proof (10th certificate)
  • Experience certificates (for SMS/STO)
8. Is there any negative marking?

Yes, the marking scheme is:

  • Prelims: 1/3 mark deduction for wrong answers
  • Mains: No negative marking
  • Unattempted questions carry no penalty
9. What is the validity of NET certificate?

NET qualification validity:

  • Lifetime validity for JRF/Lectureship
  • Must fulfill other eligibility criteria at time of appointment
  • No need to reappear for NET once qualified
10. How to prepare for ASRB exams?

Preparation strategy:

  1. Study ICAR syllabus thoroughly
  2. Solve previous years' question papers
  3. Read agricultural journals and reports
  4. Take mock tests for time management
  5. Focus on current agricultural developments
