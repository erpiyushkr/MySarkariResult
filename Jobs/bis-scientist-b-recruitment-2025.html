About Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) is the National Standards Body of India established under the BIS Act 2016. BIS is responsible for:

  • Harmonious development of standardization, marking and quality certification
  • Formulation of Indian Standards for products, processes and services
  • Certification of products for conformity to Indian Standards
  • Testing and calibration services through its network of laboratories
  • Promoting quality consciousness in the country

Why Work at BIS?

Prestigious Organization

BIS is India's national standards body working under the Ministry of Consumer Affairs, playing crucial role in quality infrastructure development.

Research Opportunities

Scientists at BIS get opportunities to work on cutting-edge standardization projects and contribute to national policy making.

International Exposure

BIS represents India in international standardization bodies like ISO and IEC, providing global exposure.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the educational qualification for BIS Scientist B posts?

The educational qualifications vary by discipline:

  • Chemistry: Master's Degree in Chemistry with minimum 60% marks (50% for SC/ST) + Valid GATE score
  • Engineering Disciplines: B.E./B.Tech in relevant branch with minimum 60% marks (50% for SC/ST) + Valid GATE score
  • GATE score must be from 2023, 2024 or 2025 examinations
2. Is work experience required for BIS Scientist B position?

No, fresh graduates can apply for Scientist B positions at BIS. The recruitment is open to:

  • Fresh graduates meeting the educational qualifications
  • Candidates with work experience (though not mandatory)
  • Final year students who will complete their qualifications before the last date of application
3. What is the career growth path for Scientist B at BIS?

The typical career progression at BIS is:

  1. Scientist B: Entry level position (Pay Level 10)
  2. Scientist C: After 4-5 years of service (Pay Level 11)
  3. Scientist D: With additional experience (Pay Level 12)
  4. Scientist E and above: Senior management positions

Promotions are based on performance and departmental examinations.

4. Can I apply for multiple disciplines in BIS Scientist B recruitment?

No, candidates can apply for only one discipline based on:

  • Their educational qualifications
  • GATE paper appeared in
  • Personal interest and expertise

Multiple applications for different disciplines will lead to cancellation of candidature.

5. What is the nature of job in BIS for Scientist B?

Scientist B at BIS typically involves:

  • Standards Formulation: Developing Indian Standards in respective fields
  • Laboratory Work: Testing and analysis of products
  • Certification: Technical evaluation for BIS certification schemes
  • Research: Conducting studies related to standardization
  • Training: Imparting training to industry stakeholders
6. Is there any interview for BIS Scientist B selection?

Yes, the selection process includes:

  1. Initial Screening: Based on GATE scores
  2. Interview: Technical and personal interview carrying 15% weightage
  3. The interview assesses subject knowledge, communication skills and aptitude for the job
  4. Candidates must qualify in both GATE score and interview separately
7. What documents are required during application?

Essential documents include:

  • Scanned photograph and signature
  • Educational qualification certificates
  • GATE scorecard (2023/24/25)
  • Caste certificate (for reserved categories)
  • PwD certificate (if applicable)
  • Experience certificates (if any)
8. What is the syllabus for the interview?

The interview will cover:

  • Technical Knowledge: Core subject based on discipline applied for
  • Standardization Concepts: Basics of standards development
  • General Awareness: Current affairs related to quality and standards
  • Aptitude: Problem solving and analytical abilities

Yes, BIS follows government norms for women reservation:

  • Gender-neutral work environment with equal opportunities
10. How can I prepare for BIS Scientist B interview?
