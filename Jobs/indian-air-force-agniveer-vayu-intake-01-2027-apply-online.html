After four years as Agniveer, up to 25% of each batch will be enrolled in the regular cadre of Indian Air Force based on performance, medical fitness, and required qualifications. Regular cadre offers permanent service with promotional avenues up to the rank of Junior Commissioned Officer and beyond.

Key Benefits & Allowances

Agniveers receive life insurance cover of ‚Çπ48 lakh, skill certification, and a substantial Seva Nidhi package upon exit. During service, they get annual leave, sick leave, and medical facilities. The corpus fund contribution (30% of package) is paid at the end of engagement.

Who Should Apply

This opportunity is ideal for young candidates (17.5-22 years) who have completed 10+2 or Diploma and wish to serve the nation in the Indian Air Force. It suits those seeking a disciplined career with adventure, training, and future opportunities in defense or civilian sectors.

Job Role & Responsibilities

Agniveer Vayu personnel perform various ground duties, including administrative, logistics, accounts, medical support, and technical tasks based on their trade. They work under the supervision of regular airmen and officers, contributing to the operational readiness of the Air Force.

How This Job Helps Society

By serving in the Indian Air Force, Agniveers directly contribute to national security and defense preparedness. They also become role models in their communities, inspiring others to join the armed forces and fostering a spirit of patriotism.

Long-Term Career Opportunities

After four years, Agniveers receive a skill certificate and financial package, enabling them to pursue higher education, vocational training, or re-employment in government/private sectors. Those selected for regular service have a full-fledged career in the IAF with pension and retirement benefits.
