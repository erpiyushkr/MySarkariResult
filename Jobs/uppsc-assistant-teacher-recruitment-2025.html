About Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission (UPPSC)

The Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission (UPPSC) is the premier recruiting agency for the state government of Uttar Pradesh. Established in 1926, UPPSC conducts examinations and selections for various civil services and administrative positions in India's most populous state.

Role of Assistant Teacher

Assistant Teachers play a vital role in the education system of Uttar Pradesh, responsible for:

  • Teaching students in grades 6-10 according to curriculum standards
  • Preparing lesson plans and educational materials
  • Assessing student performance and providing feedback
  • Maintaining classroom discipline and a positive learning environment
  • Participating in school activities and parent-teacher meetings
  • Identifying and supporting students with special needs

Career Insight:

An Assistant Teacher position offers a stable career in education with opportunities for professional development and promotions to senior teaching positions or administrative roles in the education department.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is there any interview for UPPSC Assistant Teacher selection?

No, there is no interview for UPPSC Assistant Teacher selection. The selection is based solely on the written examination marks. Candidates who qualify the written exam will be called for document verification.

2. Can candidates from other states apply for UPPSC Assistant Teacher?

Yes, candidates from other states can apply for UPPSC Assistant Teacher positions. However, they will be considered under the General category and won't be eligible for UP state reservation benefits.

3. What is the probation period for UP Assistant Teachers?

The probation period for UP Assistant Teachers is typically 2 years. During this period, candidates undergo orientation training and their performance is evaluated. Confirmation in service is subject to satisfactory completion of probation.

4. Is B.Ed mandatory for UPPSC Assistant Teacher recruitment?

Yes, B.Ed is mandatory for Assistant Teacher positions in Uttar Pradesh. Candidates must possess a Bachelor of Education degree from a recognized university along with a graduate degree in the relevant subject.

5. What is the selection ratio for UPPSC Assistant Teacher?

The selection ratio varies each year based on vacancies and applicants. Typically, about 5-7% of applicants are finally selected. The selection is purely merit-based, with cutoff marks varying by category.

6. What is the training period for selected candidates?

Selected candidates undergo a mandatory training program of 6 months at State Institute of Education centers. The training includes:

  • Pedagogical techniques
  • Classroom management
  • Curriculum implementation
  • Evaluation methods

7. Can candidates apply for multiple subjects?

No, candidates can apply for only one subject as per their educational qualifications. Multiple applications for different subjects will lead to disqualification.

8. What is the posting policy for UP Assistant Teachers?

Selected candidates can be posted anywhere in Uttar Pradesh based on vacancy positions. The initial posting is typically in government secondary schools. Candidates must serve in the allocated school for a minimum period of 3 years before requesting transfer.

9. Is there any reservation for female candidates?

Yes, Uttar Pradesh government provides 20% horizontal reservation for female candidates across all categories. This means 20% of seats in each category (General, OBC, SC, ST) are reserved for women candidates from that category.

10. What are the career progression opportunities?

UP Assistant Teachers have a structured career path:

  1. Assistant Teacher
  2. Senior Teacher (after 5-7 years of service)
  3. Head Teacher
  4. Block Education Officer
Teachers can also pursue higher qualifications like M.Ed for career advancement.
