About Intelligence Bureau and ACIO Role

What is Intelligence Bureau?

The Intelligence Bureau (IB) is India's internal intelligence agency, operating under the Ministry of Home Affairs. Established in 1887, it is responsible for gathering intelligence within India and executing counter-intelligence and counter-terrorism tasks. The IB plays a crucial role in maintaining national security and internal stability.

Role of Assistant Central Intelligence Officer (ACIO)

ACIOs are responsible for collecting and analyzing intelligence information critical to national security. Key responsibilities include:

  • Gathering intelligence from various sources
  • Analyzing security threats and preparing reports
  • Maintaining confidential records and databases
  • Coordinating with other security agencies
  • Conducting background verification of sensitive personnel
  • Monitoring activities that may threaten internal security

Career Insight:

An ACIO position offers a challenging and prestigious career in national security. Officers receive specialized training and have opportunities for advancement to higher positions within the intelligence community.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is there any physical test for IB ACIO selection?

No, there is no physical test for IB ACIO selection. However, selected candidates must undergo a medical examination to ensure they meet the required health standards for the position.

2. Can candidates from all states apply for IB ACIO?

Yes, candidates from all states and union territories of India are eligible to apply for IB ACIO positions, provided they meet the educational and age criteria. The recruitment is conducted on an all-India basis.

3. What is the probation period for IB ACIO?

The probation period for IB ACIO is typically 2 years. During this period, candidates undergo training and their performance is evaluated. Confirmation in service is subject to satisfactory completion of probation.

4. Are there any restrictions on applying for multiple positions?

Candidates can apply for only one position in the IB ACIO recruitment. Multiple applications from the same candidate will lead to disqualification.

5. What is the selection ratio for IB ACIO?

The selection ratio varies each year, but typically about 1% of applicants are finally selected. The approximate selection stages are:

  • Tier I: 20-25% qualify for Tier II
  • Tier II: 10-15% qualify for interview
  • Final selection: About 5-8% of interviewed candidates

6. What is the training period for selected candidates?

Selected candidates undergo a rigorous training program of 6-9 months at IB training facilities. The training includes:

  • Intelligence gathering techniques
  • Security protocols
  • Report writing
  • Field operations
  • Legal aspects of intelligence work

7. Can women apply for IB ACIO positions?

Yes, women are encouraged to apply for IB ACIO positions. There are no gender-based restrictions in the recruitment process. Female candidates receive the same salary and benefits as their male counterparts.

8. What is the job posting policy for IB ACIO?

IB ACIOs can be posted anywhere in India based on organizational requirements. The initial posting is typically at IB headquarters or regional offices, but field postings are common. Transfers are a regular feature of the job.

9. Is there any interview preparation guidance?

For the interview stage:

  • Prepare for questions on current national and international affairs
  • Be ready to discuss your understanding of national security challenges
  • Review basic concepts of intelligence work
  • Practice situational judgment questions
  • Be prepared for psychological assessment

10. What are the career progression opportunities?

IB ACIOs have a structured career path:

  1. Assistant Central Intelligence Officer (ACIO)
  2. Deputy Central Intelligence Officer (DCIO)
  3. Central Intelligence Officer (CIO)
  4. Assistant Director
  5. Deputy Director
  6. Joint Director
Promotions are based on performance, experience, and departmental examinations.
