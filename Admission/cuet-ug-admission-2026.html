CUET UG Admission 2026 – Eligibility, Dates, Exam Pattern & How to Apply

Complete guide for Class 12 students seeking admission to central universities through CUET UG 2026.

By MySarkariResult Team | Last Updated: 03 March 2026 | 10:00 AM

Introduction: What is CUET UG 2026?

CUET UG (Common University Entrance Test for Undergraduate) is a national-level entrance examination conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to various undergraduate programs in central universities and other participating institutions across India. Launched to bring uniformity and equal opportunity, CUET UG replaces multiple university-specific entrance tests, making the admission process simpler and more transparent.

This exam is the gateway for Class 12 students seeking admission to esteemed universities like Delhi University, JNU, BHU, Jamia Millia Islamia, and many others. With CUET UG 2026, you can apply to multiple universities and courses through a single application form. The exam tests your knowledge in languages, domain-specific subjects, and general aptitude. It’s an opportunity to secure a seat in a top central university and build a bright academic future.

Quick Overview: CUET UG 2026 will be held in computer-based mode. Candidates can choose up to 3 domain subjects and one language. The score is accepted by over 250 universities. Apply once, get considered everywhere!

Eligibility Criteria for CUET UG 2026 (Detailed)

  • Academic Qualification: Passed Class 12 or equivalent from a recognized board (CBSE, ICSE, State Boards, etc.) in 2025, 2026, or appearing in 2026. Students from open schooling are also eligible if their board is recognized.
  • Subject Requirements: Must have studied the subjects in Class 12 that you wish to appear for in CUET (domain subjects). Some universities may require specific subjects for particular courses (e.g., Physics, Chemistry, Maths for B.Sc. Physics).
  • Minimum Marks: There is no minimum aggregate prescribed by NTA. However, individual universities may set their own eligibility criteria (e.g., 45% for General, 40% for reserved). Always check the specific university's requirements.
  • Age Limit: No upper age limit for CUET UG. Candidates of any age can apply.
  • Reserved Category Relaxation: SC/ST/OBC/EWS/PwD candidates get age and mark relaxation as per university norms. Certificate must be valid and issued by competent authority.

⚠️ Note: Some universities may have additional prerequisites (e.g., Mathematics for Economics). Always read the detailed information brochure of the university you are targeting.

Marking Scheme: +5 for correct answer, -1 for incorrect answer. Unanswered questions get no marks. The exam is computer-based (online).

  • Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, Computer Science, etc.

Course duration is typically 3 years for most programs (4 years for some integrated/technical courses). Degree awarded by the respective central university. Fees vary from university to university, but central universities are generally affordable (₹5,000–₹20,000 per annum for most courses). Scholarships are available for meritorious and reserved category students.


Admission Process: How to Apply for CUET UG 2026

  1. Registration: Visit the official NTA CUET website (cuet.nta.nic.in). Click on "New Registration" and fill in basic details (name, email, mobile). A registration number and password will be sent.
  2. Fill Application Form: Log in and complete the online form: personal details, educational qualifications, choice of exam city, language, domain subjects (max 3), and general test (if desired).
  3. Upload Documents: Scan and upload recent passport-size photograph (JPEG, 10-200 KB), signature (JPEG, 4-30 KB), and category certificate (if applicable).
  4. Pay Application Fee: Pay online via debit/credit card, net banking, or UPI. Fee: General – ₹650 for up to 3 subjects, OBC/EWS – ₹600, SC/ST/PwD – ₹550. Extra subjects cost additional.
  5. Preview & Submit: Verify all details carefully, as corrections are allowed only during the correction window. Submit the form and take a printout for future reference.
  6. Download Admit Card: In May 2026, download admit card from the website using registration number and password.
  7. Appear for Exam: Report to the exam center on the allotted date and time. Carry admit card and a valid photo ID.
  8. Check Result: Result will be announced in June. Scorecard can be downloaded.
  9. Counseling & Seat Allotment: Participate in university-specific or centralized counseling (if applicable). Choose your preferred college and course based on your rank. Pay seat acceptance fee and complete document verification.
  10. Reporting to College: After seat allotment, report to the allotted college for final admission and commencement of classes.

  • Income certificate (if applying for fee waiver)

Common Mistakes Students Make & How to Avoid Them

  • ❌ Not reading eligibility carefully: Many students apply for subjects they haven't studied in Class 12. Always check domain subject eligibility.
  • ❌ Waiting till the last date to apply: Last-minute server rush can cause errors or failure to submit. Apply at least a week early.
  • ❌ Wrong document upload: Blurry photos or mismatched signatures lead to rejection. Follow size and format guidelines strictly.
  • ❌ Incorrect personal details: Mistakes in name, date of birth, or mobile number can cause issues later. Double-check before final submit.
  • ❌ Not checking official notification: Relying on unverified sources may give outdated info. Always read the official NTA brochure.
  • ❌ Ignoring the correction window: If you notice an error, use the correction window to fix it. Don't assume it's too late.
  • ❌ Not attempting mock tests: Without practice, you may struggle with time management. Take free mock tests available on the NTA website.

Student Guidance & Career Advice

How to Prepare Effectively for CUET UG

Start at least 4–6 months before the exam. First, download the syllabus from NTA website and understand the structure. For languages, practice reading comprehension and grammar. For domain subjects, stick to NCERT textbooks (Class 11 and 12) as most questions are based on them. Solve previous years' CUET papers (available on NTA site) and take online mock tests to improve speed and accuracy.

Best Books & Resources

  • NCERT textbooks (for domain subjects) – must read.
  • CUET UG preparation books by Arihant, Disha Experts (for practice sets).
  • Online platforms: NTA's official mock tests, YouTube channels for free lectures.
  • For general test: practice logical reasoning, current affairs, and numerical ability from competitive exam books.

Time Management Tips

Create a daily study schedule allocating time for each subject. Take short breaks every hour. Focus on weak areas first. In the exam, start with the sections you are most confident in. Don't spend too much time on one question. Keep at least 5 minutes for revision.

Subject Selection Advice

Choose domain subjects that you have studied in Class 12 and are comfortable with. Don't pick subjects just because they seem scoring. Also consider the courses you want to pursue: for B.A. Economics, you may need Math; for B.Sc. Biology, you need Biology. Check university-specific requirements before finalizing.

How to Reduce Exam Stress

Regular exercise, meditation, and proper sleep help. Avoid last-minute cramming. Trust your preparation. On exam day, reach the center early, carry all documents, and stay calm. Remember, CUET is just one step – many opportunities await.

Parental Guidance Tips

Parents can support by providing a quiet study environment, encouraging healthy routines, and helping with registration formalities. Avoid pressuring the child about a specific college; instead, discuss multiple options and keep communication open.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) – CUET UG 2026

1. What is CUET UG 2026?

CUET UG is a common entrance test for undergraduate admissions in central universities across India, conducted by NTA. It replaces multiple university-specific exams.

2. Who can apply for CUET UG 2026?

Students who have passed Class 12 in 2025 or 2026, or are appearing in 2026, from a recognized board. There is no age limit.

3. What is the last date to apply for CUET UG 2026?

The expected last date is 31 March 2026. Check official website for exact dates.

4. What is the exam pattern for CUET UG 2026?

It's a computer-based test with multiple-choice questions. It has four sections: languages, domain subjects, and general test. Marking: +5 for correct, -1 for incorrect.

5. Is there negative marking in CUET UG?

Yes, 1 mark is deducted for each wrong answer.

6. How many times can I attempt CUET UG?

There is no attempt limit. You can appear every year you meet eligibility.

7. What documents are required for CUET UG application?

Scanned photo, signature, Class 10/12 mark sheets (if passed), category certificate (if applicable), and a valid ID.

8. Can arts students apply for CUET UG?

Yes, arts students can choose domain subjects like History, Political Science, etc., based on their Class 12 background.

9. What is the career scope after CUET UG?

You can pursue higher studies, government jobs, private sector employment, or research. A central university degree adds value.

10. Is CUET compulsory for central university admission?

Yes, for most central universities, CUET UG is mandatory. Some institutions may have additional requirements; check their websites.

11. What is the application fee for CUET UG 2026?

General: ₹650 for up to 3 subjects; OBC/EWS: ₹600; SC/ST: ₹550; Third gender: ₹550. Additional subjects cost extra.

12. How can I prepare for CUET UG effectively?

Understand the syllabus, solve previous years' papers, take mock tests, and manage your time. Focus on NCERT books for domain subjects.
